HOME TOWN

Cabramatta

Cabramatta is located in south-western Sydney and is a part of the City of Fairfield Council. More known as Cabra, it is the largest Vietnamese community in Australia and is scattered with many asian cuisine places from many countires such as Vietnam, Thailand, China and Cambodia. I have been living here my whole life and its well known for its melting pot of culture and cuisine. Since the crackdown on gangs and drugs in 2002, the place has been upgraded and modernised. With each new Generation of residents Cabramatta continues to be a great place to hang out with friends and family as memorable places slowly get replaced with new faces. With the help of community projects and the government, it has become a good tourist place for asian visitors and is the host to the yearly Moon Festival.

GOUGH WHITLAM PLACE

GOUGH WHITLAM PLACE

On June 2016, in Dutton Plaza, a three-storey building was built and Fairfield Council funded and bought retail development by replacing the existing council owned car pack. In doing so, 31 retail places were opened up with the first floor being four offices and the second and third floors being extra car park for residents and workers. Since the replacement of the place, the traffic has significantly improved and many people have maintained very successful and memorable businesses there. The public open space at the front entrance was name Gough Whitlam Place in honour of Gough Whitlam who represented Cabramatta. many cafes have been established there and many older aged citizens come together to watch each other play Chinese Chess. Since the establishment of this venue, Cabramatta has become more popular and more attractive overall.

JOHN STREET

JOHN STREET

John Street is a famous street in Cabramatta that has a one way road connecting towards the Cumberland Highway. Along the street is countless numbers of restaurants, banks, cafes, tea shops and commercial shops. It has numerous Vietnamese, Chinese and Cambodian cuisine with different styles and vibes. More branded shops such as Sushi Hub, Bread Top, Gong Cha and Red Lee also exist there and serves to contribute to the variety of flavours Cabramatta has to offer.

MOON FESTIVAL

MOON FESTIVAL

The Cabramatta Moon Festival is a yearly tradition that celebrates the full moon with a variety of spectacular events. The date is determined by the equinox in which the length of day and night are equal. During the festival, many streets that connect within Cabramatta are shut off and are filled with stalls selling a wide variety of traditional accessories, clothes, toys, treats and bags for everyone to enjoy. Some stall give out free food symbolising the diversity and vitality of the asian culture. During this time, many performances can occur such as the traditional lion dancing, musical performances, stunts from Parkour/Tricking communities INVINCIBLE group and Team9Lives as well as traditional dancing. They also hire numerous playplaces and rides for children and the family to enjoy. To end of the day, a spectacular collection of fireworks are displayed signalling the reckoning of luckiness, wealth and good health. The Moon Festival is the biggest celebration of Cabramatta and has heavily fueled its reputation and made it a very welcoming place to visit and live in.
